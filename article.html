State Rolls Out New Blue Line Rail Schedule for Commuters

The expanded timetable adds more coaches and earlier morning departures, as officials push to ease the mainland-to-island commute ahead of the festive season.

By Gistarena247 Desk 4 hours ago 6 min read

The state transport authority confirmed on Monday that the Blue Line rail service will move to a denser schedule starting next week, with trains running every 12 minutes during peak hours instead of the current 20.

Officials say the change follows months of feedback from daily commuters who described overcrowding on the mainland-to-island route, particularly in the early mornings when demand is highest.

Alongside the new timetable, four additional coaches are being added to the existing fleet, with two more scheduled to enter service before the end of the year. Fare prices remain unchanged for now, though officials say a review is planned for the next quarter.

Commuter groups have welcomed the update but say more stations along the line need better lighting and covered waiting areas, especially during the rainy season. The authority says it is reviewing station upgrades as part of a wider infrastructure plan.
